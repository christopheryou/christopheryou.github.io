  • Embodiment & Agency of Virtual Avatars

    A Sense of Urgency on the Sense of Agency: Challenges in Evaluating Agency and Embodiment in Virtual Reality

    June 2022 - October 2024

    Virtual Reality

    This project investigates the relationship between user control and the sense of agency in virtual reality (VR) environments, specifically how these factors impact the sense of embodiment. While embodiment in VR has been widely studied, measuring the subjective sense of agencyâ€”how much control users feel they have over their avatarsâ€”remains challenging and inconsistent. Through a study where participants control avatars with varying degrees of movement (head only, head and torso, and full body), the research demonstrates that while increased control enhances embodiment, current metrics for measuring agency are inadequate. The study aims to improve understanding of how control and agency interact, contributing to the broader field of VR and cognitive performance research.

    Technologies: Unity, Python

    Articles: A Sense of Urgency on the Sense of Agency: Challenges in Evaluating Agency and Embodiment in Virtual Reality

  • Virtual Agents in Mental Health Conversations

    Virtual Human Motivational Interviews to Improve Readiness to Change Mental Health

    June 2024

    Virtual Agents

    This project explores the use of generative AI-driven virtual humans (VHs) to enhance engagement in online mental health interventions through motivational interviewing. While asynchronous online mental health platforms offer accessibility and flexibility, they often lack the emotional connection needed to foster user commitment. By leveraging GAI, virtual humans can simulate more natural, adaptive conversations, emulating human therapists to create personalized, empathetic interactions. This study investigates how AI-powered VHs impact users' readiness for change, acceptance of mental health interventions, and trust in the therapeutic process, aiming to improve the effectiveness of online mental health resources.

    Technologies: Node, JavaScript, CSS, AWS, OpenAI, three.js, Machine Learning Models, Python, FastAPI

    Articles: N/A

  • Clinical Trial Enrollment Project

    R24: Clinical Trial Enrollment Project

    May 2021 - Current

    Virtual Agents, AI/ML

    This NIH-funded project seeks to use Virtual Human Technology to increase clinical trial participation among older adult minorities (Black/African American, Hispanic/Latin, and/or rural adults over the age of 50). We designed the virtual human ALEX to be culturally sensitive, track engagement in NIH-funded trials, and compare ALEX and standard recruitment methods. Clinical trials are queried and summarized using prompting techniques with LLMs in real-time w/ LLM (RAG) chatbot support.

    Technologies: HTML, JavaScript, CSS, Unreal Engine, MetaHumans, Machine Learning Models, Python, ChatGPT
